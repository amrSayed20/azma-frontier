# AZMA Technology Adoption Constitution

## Constitutional Status

This document defines the mandatory governance system for technology adoption decisions in AZMA OS.

It is a constitutional architecture reference only.

No implementation code, runtime module creation, or infrastructure deployment is authorized by this document.

## 1. Technology Adoption Requirements

A technology may be adopted only if all conditions are satisfied:

1. Passed the Research Evaluation Constitution with acceptable constitutional score band.
2. Passed all hard constraints.
3. Completed trial and proof-of-concept process.
4. Completed benchmark approval process.
5. Produced explicit adoption rationale, risk profile, and exit path.
6. Received constitutional approvals through required workflow.

## 2. Technology Rejection Requirements

A technology must be rejected when one or more are true:

1. Hard-constraint failure in security, privacy, provider independence, self-hosting, or DNA compatibility.
2. Inability to meet constitutional governance requirements.
3. Licensing or legal incompatibility with sovereign deployment.
4. Strategic risk exceeds approved tolerance without accepted mitigation.
5. Trial or benchmark evidence is non-reproducible or materially contradictory.

## 3. Trial and Proof-of-Concept Process

1. Define trial charter with objective, hypothesis, success criteria, and boundaries.
2. Execute controlled proof-of-concept under constitutional constraints.
3. Capture repeatable evidence, assumptions, and limitations.
4. Produce trial verdict: advance, defer, or reject.

Trial does not imply adoption approval.

## 4. Benchmark Approval Process

Every trial must include comparative benchmarks:

1. Baseline candidate vs evaluated candidate(s).
2. Standardized workload definitions.
3. Measurement of quality, latency, throughput, cost, and reliability.
4. Reproducibility validation.
5. Confidence scoring and variance reporting.

Benchmark results are approved only when methodology is comparable and independently reproducible.

## 5. Constitutional Approval Workflow

1. Research submitter provides evaluation, trial, and benchmark package.
2. Architectural Review verifies constitutional and boundary compliance.
3. Security and Privacy Review validates risk controls.
4. Sovereign High Council review issues governance decision.
5. Founder approval gate is applied when required.
6. Chief Architect ratifies final constitutional decision.

No stage may be bypassed.

### Official Adoption Condition

A technology becomes an official part of AZMA OS only after:

1. completion of sections 1 through 5,
2. required Founder and Sovereign High Council gates are satisfied,
3. Chief Architect ratification is formally recorded.

## 6. Founder Approval Gates

Founder approval is mandatory when adoption affects:

1. Core constitutional governance behavior.
2. Provider dependency concentration risk.
3. High-cost infrastructure commitments.
4. Strategic AI direction of the Empire.
5. Irreversible architectural migration paths.

## 7. Sovereign High Council Approval Gates

Sovereign High Council must approve:

1. Adoption rationale and tradeoffs.
2. Risk profile and mitigation plan.
3. Constitutional compatibility statement.
4. Exit and rollback readiness.
5. Re-evaluation schedule.

## 8. Risk Acceptance Policy

Risk acceptance is governed by constitutional thresholds:

1. Low risk may proceed through standard approval workflow.
2. Medium risk requires explicit mitigation commitments.
3. High risk requires Sovereign High Council acceptance and Founder gate review.
4. Critical risk is non-acceptable unless constitutional exception is explicitly ratified.

No implicit risk acceptance is permitted.

## 9. Re-evaluation Policy

Post-adoption technologies must be periodically re-evaluated.

Mandatory triggers:

1. Security/privacy incidents.
2. Major pricing changes.
3. Performance regressions.
4. Better strategic alternatives emerging.
5. Constitutional or DNA compatibility concerns.

Re-evaluation outcomes: continue, constrain, migrate, or retire.

## 10. Technology Retirement Policy

A technology must be retired when it becomes constitutionally non-compliant or strategically harmful.

Retirement protocol:

1. Retirement Notice with rationale and risk impact.
2. Controlled transition plan.
3. Data and operational continuity safeguards.
4. Replacement validation.
5. Final retirement ratification by governance workflow.

## 11. Emergency Rollback Policy

Emergency rollback is mandatory when active technology introduces immediate constitutional, security, or operational risk.

Emergency rollback rules:

1. Trigger conditions must be pre-defined in adoption records.
2. Rollback authority follows constitutional approval hierarchy.
3. Rollback execution must preserve continuity and audit trace.
4. Post-rollback review is mandatory before reinstatement.

## 12. Version Governance for Adopted Technologies

Every adopted technology must be version-governed.

Version governance requirements:

1. Approved version baseline must be recorded at ratification.
2. Upgrades require constitutional impact review.
3. Major version changes require re-evaluation workflow.
4. Deprecated versions require migration or retirement decision.
5. Version history must remain traceable in governance records.

## Final Constitutional Statement

No technology enters or remains in AZMA OS without constitutional adoption governance under this document.
