# AZMA Research Study 03 - Video Generation Ecosystem

## Study Status

This is a constitutional research study artifact only.

No implementation, no benchmarking, no ranking, no winner selection, and no adoption recommendation are included.

## Objective

Build a comprehensive high-level constitutional knowledge base of the modern video-generation ecosystem relevant to AZMA OS, covering open-source and commercial pathways across self-hosted and API-based operating models.

## Category Classification

### 1) Foundation Video Generation Models
- Purpose: General-purpose video synthesis across broad prompt and style requirements.
- General strengths: Broad capability coverage, flexible generation pathways, adaptable creative range.
- General weaknesses: Resource intensity, consistency drift over long temporal spans.
- Typical use cases: Story ideation, cinematic concept generation, campaign visuals.
- Self-hosting capability: Medium to high.
- Licensing model: Mixed open/commercial/restricted.
- Ecosystem maturity: Medium to high.
- Community activity: High.
- Enterprise suitability: Medium to high.
- Suitability for AZMA OS: High as a core generation layer.

### 2) Image-to-Video Systems
- Purpose: Animate still images into temporally coherent motion clips.
- General strengths: Strong for rapid animation from existing assets and concept boards.
- General weaknesses: Motion realism variance and scene continuity limitations.
- Typical use cases: Asset animation, teaser clips, storyboard motion previews.
- Self-hosting capability: Medium to high.
- Licensing model: Mixed.
- Ecosystem maturity: Medium to high.
- Community activity: High.
- Enterprise suitability: High for fast media workflows.
- Suitability for AZMA OS: High for chamber-driven visual pipelines.

### 3) Text-to-Video Systems

- Self-hosting capability: Medium.
