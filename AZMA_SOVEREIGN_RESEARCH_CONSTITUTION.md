# AZMA Sovereign Research Constitution

## Constitutional Status

This document establishes the Phase 1 constitutional foundation of the Sovereign Research Program in AZMA OS.

It is a constitutional architecture reference only.

No implementation code, runtime module construction, infrastructure provisioning, or research framework execution is authorized by this document.

## 1. Mission

The Sovereign Research Program exists to identify, verify, and constitutionally qualify future technologies before architectural adoption, so AZMA OS evolves through evidence instead of trend pressure.

Its mission is to reduce strategic uncertainty, prevent premature dependency lock-in, and protect long-term sovereignty of the Empire.

## 2. Vision

Sovereign Research enables AZMA OS to become a permanently adaptive yet identity-preserving civilization-scale architecture where every major technology decision is:

1. constitutionally aligned,
2. provider-agnostic,
3. evidence-backed,
4. reversible when required,
5. optimized for long-term strategic independence.

## 3. Scope

### Included in Scope

1. Discovery and constitutional analysis of AI models, toolchains, and ecosystem platforms.
2. Research on multimodal generation domains: text, image, audio, voice, and video technologies.
3. Research on GPU execution strategy, queue optimization, streaming generation, and progressive rendering approaches.
4. Comparative study of open-source projects, model repositories, scientific papers, and emerging AI organizations.
5. Strategic evaluation of licensing, maintainability, ecosystem maturity, and sovereignty risk.

### Explicitly Excluded from Scope

1. Production implementation.
2. Runtime integration.
3. Infrastructure deployment.
4. Dependency installation or migration.
5. Feature development.
6. Any operational launch activity.

## 4. Architectural Principles

The following principles are immutable for all future research activity:

1. Constitutional Supremacy: research may inform decisions but may never bypass constitutional law.
2. Sovereignty First: no recommendation may weaken strategic independence.
3. Provider Agnosticism: no recommendation may force irreversible single-vendor dependence.
4. Evidence Over Opinion: findings must be reproducible, traceable, and auditable.
5. Reversibility by Design: recommendations must preserve exit and migration paths.
6. Boundary Integrity: research outputs may not alter runtime boundaries or module ownership.
7. Long-Horizon Orientation: decisions must optimize multi-year resilience, not short-term novelty.

## 5. Research Governance

### Proposal Authority

Research may be proposed by:

1. Chief Architect,
2. Sovereign High Council mandate,
3. designated constitutional intelligence layers,
4. Founder strategic directive.

### Research Execution Authority

Research is performed by the designated Sovereign Research function operating under Chief Architect constitutional doctrine.

### Review Authority

Research quality and constitutional compliance are reviewed by the constitutional architecture review function before recommendation is eligible for Founder Review.

### Evaluation Authority

Evaluation is performed by the authorized constitutional research function under Chief Architect standards.

### Approval Authority

1. Chief Architect ratifies architectural validity.
2. Founder approves high-impact strategic directions.
3. Sovereign High Council formalizes governance acceptance.

### Rejection Authority

Research proposals or recommendations may be rejected by:

1. Chief Architect for architectural non-compliance,
2. Sovereign High Council for governance non-alignment,
3. Founder for strategic misalignment.

### Official Decision Formation

A research decision becomes official only when:

1. evaluation is complete,
2. governance review is complete,
3. approval/rejection is recorded through constitutional authority,
4. decision rationale is archived as sovereign knowledge.

## 6. Research Lifecycle

The Sovereign Research Lifecycle is constitutionally fixed as:

Idea

↓

Research

↓

Evidence Collection

↓

Evaluation

↓

Recommendation

↓

Founder Review

↓

Approval / Rejection

↓

Knowledge Archive

### Lifecycle Stage Definitions

1. Idea: strategic problem or opportunity is declared.
2. Research: evidence and candidate landscape are collected.
3. Evidence Collection: all claims are supported by verifiable sources and traceable records.
4. Evaluation: candidates are assessed under constitutional criteria.
5. Recommendation: ranked options and tradeoffs are documented.
6. Founder Review: sovereign strategic review occurs.
7. Approval / Rejection: constitutional decision is ratified.
8. Knowledge Archive: full decision memory is preserved for future governance.

## 7. Relationship with AZMA DNA

All research outcomes must remain compatible with AZMA DNA architecture.

No recommendation is constitutional if it compromises:

1. provider-agnostic orchestration,
2. replaceable execution pathways,
3. clean ownership boundaries,
4. long-term sovereign evolvability.

Research is subordinate to DNA identity constraints and must strengthen, not dilute, constitutional architecture.

## 8. Relationship with the Living Constitution

Sovereign Research is a support system to the Living Constitution, not an authority above it.

Research cannot override constitutional principles, governance doctrines, or identity protections.

If a research finding conflicts with constitutional law, the finding is invalid for adoption until the conflict is resolved constitutionally.

## 9. Research Evidence Standard

Every recommendation must be supported by verifiable evidence.

Acceptable evidence includes:

1. official documentation,
2. open-source repositories,
3. source code inspection,
4. scientific papers,
5. independent benchmark results,
6. community maturity,
7. long-term maintenance activity,
8. security history,
9. licensing analysis,
10. real production validation.

Architectural decisions must never rely only on popularity or marketing claims.

Evidence always has priority over opinion.
