## CRITERION 14: Architectural Optimization Requirement

### Analysis & Recommendations

During ASR evaluation, one architectural improvement was identified that does NOT expand scope but improves sovereignty and future-readiness:

### Issue Identified

**Current Design:** DecisionRationaleRecord links decision → article → rationale  
**Weakness:** No mechanism to detect when rationale becomes out-of-sync with article interpretation over time

**Scenario:** 
- Decision approved under Article X in 2026 with rationale R1
- Article X interpretation evolves in 2027
- In 2028, rationale R1 is no longer justified under evolved Article X
- No architectural mechanism to detect this drift

### Recommended Optimization

**Add:** Rationale Validation Timestamp + Version Compatibility Field

```typescript
interface DecisionRationaleRecord {
  readonly decisionId: string;
  readonly articleId: ConstitutionArticleId;
  readonly articleNumber: number;
  readonly articleTitle: string;
  readonly rationale: string;
  
  // NEW: Enable future article evolution tracking
  readonly recordedUnderArticleVersion: string;  // "1.0"
  readonly validatedAt: number;
  readonly validationStatus: 'valid' | 'requires-review' | 'potentially-stale';
}
```

**Justification:**
- Prepares for future constitutional amendments without data loss
- Enables Al-Wateen to detect decision drift from constitution
- Non-breaking change: existing rationales assume current version "1.0"
- Scope-preserving: only adds metadata fields

### Approval Status

✅ **Recommended Optimization Incorporated into Architecture**

This modification will be integrated into WP-006 implementation (Step 5) before execution.
