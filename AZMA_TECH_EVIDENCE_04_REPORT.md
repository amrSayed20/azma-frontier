# AZMA Technology Evidence Collection 04 Report

## Executive Summary

Technology Evidence Collection 04 for the Audio and Speech Ecosystem is complete as an evidence-only constitutional activity using primary sources.

Created:
- AZMA_TECH_EVIDENCE_04_AUDIO.md
- AZMA_TECH_EVIDENCE_04_REPORT.md

Evidence was collected for major official families across the required fixed research areas: OpenAI Audio/Realtime, Google Gemini Audio and Lyria, Microsoft Azure Speech, ElevenLabs, Deepgram, and Stability Stable Audio.

Primary-source findings (facts only):
- TTS and STT are explicitly documented across most surveyed families. OpenAI, Google, Azure, ElevenLabs, and Deepgram each publish official pathways for text-to-speech and/or speech-to-text, including request-based and realtime variants in multiple families.
- Voice cloning is explicitly evidenced in OpenAI (custom voices with consent workflow), Azure (custom voice), and ElevenLabs (instant/professional cloning). For Deepgram, Google, and Stability in this source set, explicit voice-cloning evidence was not retrieved and is marked UNKNOWN.
- Voice conversion (speech-to-speech) is explicitly evidenced in ElevenLabs (voice changer). Other families in this collection either document adjacent capabilities (for example speech translation or speech-to-speech conversational sessions) or remain UNKNOWN where dedicated conversion workflows were not explicit.
- Realtime conversational audio is explicitly evidenced in OpenAI Realtime sessions, Azure Voice Live, ElevenLabs Speech Engine, and Deepgram Voice Agent/streaming surfaces.
- Music generation evidence is explicit for Google (Lyria 3 and Lyria RealTime) and Stability Stable Audio model family and model card sources.
- SFX generation evidence is explicit for ElevenLabs Sound Effects and supported in Stability Stable Audio family positioning/model-card context.
- Audio editing/transformation evidence is uneven across families: explicit in selected pathways (for example ElevenLabs voice transformation and product-level Stability editing claims), while many families do not provide direct explicit waveform-editing evidence in retrieved sources.
- Audio enhancement/isolation is frequently undocumented in retrieved primary sources and therefore marked UNKNOWN where not explicit.
- Realtime streaming and low-latency operations are strongly evidenced across OpenAI, Google (Lyria RealTime), ElevenLabs, and Deepgram documentation sets.

All non-explicit items are marked UNKNOWN in the evidence ledger. No ranking, recommendation, benchmarking conclusion, implementation, or architectural decision was produced.
